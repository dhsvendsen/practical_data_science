'''[[R≈çnin|Ronin]]''' is an [[wikt:alias|alias]] used by multiple characters in the {{Marvel Universe}}. The alias was first adopted by [[Echo (Marvel Comics)|Echo]] in ''[[The New Avengers (comics)|New Avengers]]'' #11 (November 2005) by [[Brian Michael Bendis]] and [[Joe Quesada]].

The [[Hawkeye (Clint Barton)|Clint Barton]] incarnation of Ronin appears in the [[Marvel Cinematic Universe]] film ''[[Avengers: Endgame]]'' (2019), portrayed by [[Jeremy Renner]]. Barton's time as Ronin will also appear on the streaming television series ''[[Hawkeye (2021 TV series)|Hawkeye]]'' on [[Disney+]].

==Publication history==
The persona of Ronin was created by [[Brian Michael Bendis]] and [[Joe Quesada]]. Bendis stated that the original intention was [[Daredevil (Marvel Comics character)|Matt Murdock]] as the true identity of Ronin but could not do so due to a conflict with plans in the ''Daredevil'' comic book title.<ref>[http://goodcomics.comicbookresources.com/2008/06/12/comic-book-urban-legends-revealed-159/ Comic Book Urban Legends Revealed #159], [[Comic Book Resources]], June 12, 2008</ref>

==Fictional character biography==

===Maya Lopez===
{{main|Echo (Marvel Comics)}}
The original iteration of Ronin first appeared in ''[[The New Avengers (comics)|New Avengers]]'' #11 (November 2005) (although the character appeared on the covers of several earlier issues). [[Echo (Marvel Comics)|Maya Lopez]] was the first person to take on the Ronin identity.<ref>''New Avengers'' #11 (November 2005)</ref> 

===Clint Barton===
{{main|Hawkeye (Clint Barton)}}
The second iteration of Ronin first appeared in ''New Avengers'' #27 (April 2007). [[Hawkeye (Clint Barton)|Clinton "Clint" Barton]] is the second person to have used the Ronin identity.<ref>''New Avengers'' #27 (April 2007)</ref>

===Alexei Shostakov===
{{main|Red Guardian}}
Writer [[Jim McCann (writer)|Jim McCann]] revealed "We're going to learn that there is far more to the Ronin identity than anyone knew, pre-dating [the first two versions] before [the third iteration]. Why does Ronin exist? That's a central mystery we will tackle."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.comicbookresources.com/?page=article&id=27204 |title=McCann & Swierczynski Are "Widow Makers" |publisher=Comic Book Resources |access-date=2010-09-13}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://comics.ign.com/articles/110/1106327p1.html |title=SDCC10: Killing Hawkeye & Widow - Comics News at IGN |publisher=Comics.ign.com |date=2010-07-15 |access-date=2010-09-13}}</ref> A [[Life Model Decoy]] of [[Red Guardian|Alexei Shostakov]] is the third individual who dons the Ronin identity.<ref>''Widowmaker'' #1 (February 2011)</ref> 

===Eric Brooks===
{{main|Blade (character)}}
A character (whose identity is initially kept from the reader) is introduced in the ''[[The Mighty Avengers|Mighty Avengers]]'' and dons the Halloween-type Spider Hero costume during the ''[[Infinity (comic book)|Infinity]]'' storyline,<ref>''Mighty Avengers'' Vol. 2 #1</ref> and is supplied with the Ronin costume from a "big box of Clint Barton's old stuff" during the ''[[Inhumanity (comics)|Inhumanity]]'' storyline.<ref>''Mighty Avengers'' #4.INH (February 2014)</ref> Before being revealed in [[Canon (fiction)|canon]], a leaked script revealed the fourth iteration's true identity to be [[Blade (character)|Eric Brooks]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.comicbookresources.com/?page=article&id=48773|title=Marvel Inadvertently Leaks Mighty Avengers Mystery Member ID|last=Sunu|first=Steve|date=28 October 2013|website=Comic Book Resources|access-date=28 October 2013}}</ref><ref>''Mighty Avengers'' #9</ref>

===Bullseye===
{{main|Bullseye (character)}}
In the ''Hawkeye: Freefall'' storyline, Clint becomes Ronin once more to launch an attack on [[The Hood (comics)|the Hood's]] criminal empire. The Hood eventually hires Bullseye to find out Ronin's true identity. After learning that Clint is Ronin through his associates, the Hood then orders Bullseye to commit crimes dressed in the Ronin costume to ruin Clint's reputation.<ref>''Hawkeye: Freefall'' #6. Marvel Comics.</ref>

==Other versions==
===Ultimate Marvel===
The [[Ultimate Marvel]] equivalent of Ronin is an [[Dissociative identity disorder|alternate personality]] of '''[[Moon Knight|Marc Spector]]'''.<ref name="ultimatespiderman108">''Ultimate Spider-Man'' #108 (June 2007)</ref> In order to infiltrate the [[Kingpin (character)|Kingpin]]'s forces, Ronin proves himself by luring [[Spider-Man (Ultimate Marvel character)|Spider-Man]] into a fight. The two engage in a brutal battle while Moon Knight and Ronin fight a concurrent battle in Spector's mind. Ronin knocks Spider-Man unconscious and apparently "kills" Moon Knight before taking the youth to the Kingpin for interrogation.<ref name="ultimatespiderman108"/> However, the Kingpin attacks Ronin, revealing his knowledge of Ronin's secret identity.<ref>''Ultimate Spider-Man'' #109</ref> Two of the Kingpin's henchmen take Ronin to a river and execute him. However, Ronin survives under mysterious circumstances and goes to the police to provide evidence about the Kingpin's attempt to murder him. While he is forced to reveal his identity, the Kingpin is arrested and the media refer to Spector as a hero.<ref>''Ultimate Spider-Man'' #110</ref>

===Heroes Reborn===
In issue five of the "[[Heroes Reborn (2021 comic)|Heroes Reborn]]" miniseries, '''[[Black Panther (character)|T'Challa]]''' uses the Ronin identity to break into the [[Squadron Supreme]]'s headquarters and steal their files, only to be driven off by [[Nighthawk (Marvel Comics)|Nighthawk]].<ref>''Heroes Reborn'' (vol. 2) #5 (May 2021). Marvel Comics.</ref>

==In other media==
===Television===
An original character named '''Nozomu Akatsuki''' operates as Ronin in the anime series ''[[Marvel Disk Wars: The Avengers]]'', voiced by [[Hideyuki Hori]] in the Japanese version and [[Keith Silverstein]] in the English dub.<ref>https://www.behindthevoiceactors.com/tv-shows/Marvel-Disk-Wars-The-Avengers/Nozomu-Akatsuki/</ref> The father of Akira and Hikaru Akatsuki, Nozomu is the co-developer of the DISKS (Digital Identity Securement Kit) before becoming Ronin.  

===Marvel Cinematic Universe===
{{Main|Clint Barton (Marvel Cinematic Universe)}}
The Clint Barton incarnation of Ronin appears in live-action media set in the [[Marvel Cinematic Universe]], portrayed by [[Jeremy Renner]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://comicbook.com/marvel/2019/04/07/avengers-endgame-clint-barton-is-officially-ronin-jeremy-renner/|title=Marvel Studios Is Officially Calling Clint Barton Ronin in 'Avengers: Endgame'|work=Comic Book.com|last=Barnhardt|first=Adam|date=April 6, 2019|access-date=April 7, 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190407025611/https://comicbook.com/marvel/2019/04/07/avengers-endgame-clint-barton-is-officially-ronin-jeremy-renner/|archive-date=April 7, 2019|url-status=live}}</ref>
* He first appears in the film, ''[[Avengers: Endgame]]'' (2019). Though not called by the name, Barton dons the Ronin attire five years after his family disappears during [[the Blip]] and became a vengeful killer who targets criminals he believes do not deserve to live.
* The [[Disney+]] series ''[[Hawkeye (2021 TV series)|Hawkeye]]'' will explore Barton's time as Ronin leading up to the events of ''Avengers: Endgame'' and see him face consequences as a result of his actions following the Blip. Additionally, Maya Lopez will also appear in the series.

===Video games===
* The Maya Lopez incarnation of Ronin appears as an exclusive playable character in the [[PlayStation Portable|PSP]] version of ''[[Marvel: Ultimate Alliance]]'', voiced by [[Marabina Jaimes]]. 
* The Clint Barton incarnation of Ronin appears as an alternate suit for Barton as Hawkeye in ''[[Ultimate Marvel vs. Capcom 3]]'', ''[[Marvel Heroes (video game)|Marvel Heroes]]'' and ''[[Avengers Academy]]'' and ''[[Marvel Ultimate Alliance 3]]'', and as a playable character in ''[[Marvel Future Fight]]'', ''[[Marvel Contest of Champions]]'' and ''[[Marvel's Avengers (video game)|Marvel's Avengers]]''.
* The Eric Brooks incarnation of Ronin appears as a playable character in ''[[Lego Marvel's Avengers]]''.

===Toys===
* A [[Minimate]] of the character was released in the 12th series of Marvel Minimates, in a 2-pack with a battle damaged "Riot-Attack" variant of [[Spider-Man]].
* Marvel Legends released a 2-pack of the Clint Barton incarnation of Ronin and [[Elektra (character)|Elektra]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.oafe.net/artemis/mlhfo1_er.php |title=ML: Elektra & Ronin review |publisher=OAFE |access-date=2010-09-13}}</ref>
* Marvel Universe released a Ronin figure.
